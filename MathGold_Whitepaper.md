# MathGold: A Hybrid Proof-of-Work Blockchain for Useful Mathematics

## Abstract
MathGold is a decentralized ledger that utilizes a hybrid 50/50 consensus model, splitting computational effort between traditional cryptographic hashing (SHA-256) and Useful Proof-of-Work (UPoW) bounties.

## Section X: The Projected Proof-of-Work Portfolio

To ensure a robust, secure, and economically viable transition toward Useful Proof-of-Work (UPoW), the MathGold consensus mechanism will initially employ a hybrid 50/50 portfolio distribution. This split anchors the network's security in established, highly liquid cryptographic hashing while systematically channeling immense computational power into humanity's most challenging mathematical problems.

### 1. The Base Layer: 50% Bitcoin (SHA-256)
Despite standard cryptographic hashing generating no extrinsic scientific utility, it provides an indispensable "base case" for network security.
* **Economic Anchor**: Maintaining a 50% allocation to traditional SHA-256 hashing guarantees baseline security, liquidity, and immediate integration with existing mining hardware (ASICs). 
* **Sybil Resistance**: It ensures the network remains highly resistant to 51% attacks even if the novel mathematical bounties experience periods of low computational liquidity or take time to attract specialized solvers.

### 2. Useful Mathematical Work: 50% MathGold
The remaining 50% of the block rewards and computational expenditure is dedicated to solving rigorously formalized mathematical and computational problems. This half of the portfolio is dynamically balanced among several distinct pillars of discovery:

#### A. Formalized Error-Correcting Codes (SDECCs)
* **Allocation**: ~10%
* **Objective**: Finding optimal bounds, codebook generations, and formal proofs for Symmetric Deletion Error-Correcting Codes (e.g., Varshamov-Tenengolts codes).
* **Utility**: Directly applicable to genetic sequencing, deep-space communication, and decentralized data storage where insertion/deletion errors are prevalent.

#### B. Prime Hunting (Mersenne Primes)
* **Allocation**: ~10%
* **Objective**: Discovering new Mersenne primes ($2^p - 1$) and verifying primality using deterministic algorithms (e.g., Lucas-Lehmer) translated into formal Lean proofs.
* **Utility**: Advances number theory and provides massive stress-testing for computational hardware.

#### C. Conjecture Counterexamples
* **Allocation**: ~10%
* **Objective**: Computational searches for counterexamples to outstanding mathematical conjectures (e.g., Euler's sum of powers, Collatz trajectories, Riemann zeta roots).
* **Utility**: Accelerates the resolution of historic problems by incentivizing brute-force and heuristic search spaces that are typically underfunded in academia.

#### D. Perfect Information Game Trees (Chess & Go)
* **Allocation**: ~10%
* **Objective**: Finding the strict upper bound on the number of steps required for a guaranteed win (or draw) from the starting position in Chess and Go.
* **Utility**: Pushes the boundaries of combinatorial game theory, retrograde analysis, and artificial intelligence optimization techniques by solving the ultimate game trees.

#### E. The Mathematician's Proposal Fund (Dynamic Adaptation)
* **Allocation**: ~10%
* **Objective**: A flexible, governance-driven bucket reserved for "Bounties of Eminence"—specific computational challenges formally proposed by leading mathematicians and research institutions.
* **Utility**: Ensures the MathGold protocol remains agile and continuously aligned with the most pressing needs of the modern scientific community.
