⚙️ The Chaotic Compressor (T1 & T2)

In SHA-256, the true cryptographic security comes from the chaotic addition of intermediate values inside two compressor functions: T1 and T2. Let's break down the math for the round you clicked:

What is a "Rotate"?

In a 2-bit system, there are only two positions: a left bit and a right bit. A rotate shifts bits right, wrapping them back to the left edge.

Ch & Maj

  • Choose (Ch): Uses E's bits to act as a switch, picking bits from either F or G.
  • Majority (Maj): Looks at A, B, and C bit-by-bit, and outputs whatever bit value (0 or 1) is in the majority for that specific position.

K & W

  • Round Constants (K): Constants unique to each round, adding pseudo-random asymmetry to the algorithm.
  • Message Schedule (W): The actual message blocks (e.g. transaction data) being hashed, broken into chunks.
